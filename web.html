BOREAL FOREST ( TAIGA )

Boreal forests, also known as taiga, are the largest land-based biome on Earth, characterized by long, cold winters and short, cool summers. They are dominated by conifer species like spruce, fir, and pine, with some deciduous trees like birch and aspen. Boreal forests span across North America, Europe, and Asia, and are crucial for regulating global climate through carbon storage and their impact on land surface albedo
